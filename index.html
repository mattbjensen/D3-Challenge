Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

by MATTHEW B. JENSEN

New links between health risks and measures of age and income have been discovered by data analysts across the United States! This breaking news summary highlights the salient facts below:

Obesity
- With the stongest correlation yet, obesity and median household income have a clearly visible negative correlation. To clarify, obesity rates are lower in states with a higher median household income.
- As related to poverty, a slightly positive correlation exists between states' obesity rates and the percentange of the states' population living in poverty.
- Age and Obesity do not appear to have a meaningful correlation at the state level.

Smoking
- Smoking and poverty have a visible positive correlation.
- Regarding smoking and age, most states fall within a ten year age range for the median smoker's age. Just four states have median soker ages falling outside of ages 32 to 42 in the entire dataset.
- Similar to obesity and median household income, we see a strong negative correlation between the percentange of smokers in a state and the median household income.

Healthcare
- Also of note, we see a strong positive correlation between the percentange of residents lacking healthcare and the percentange of residents in poverty.
- Age group seems to show no reasonable correlation, and most datapoints fall between 32 and 42.
- Though weaker than the correlations of smoking and obesity to median household income, the percentange of residents lacking healthcare still show a visible negative correlation to median household income by state.
